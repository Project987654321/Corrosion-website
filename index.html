Theory of Corrosion

Corrosion is the destruction of metal through unwanted or unintentional chemical or electrochemical reaction which occurs between the surface of the metal and the environment.

  • Causes: ores, extraction process, natural tendency of metal
  • Consequences: equipment failure, safety hazards, maintenance cost

Dry/Atmospheric Corrosion:

Reaction of metals with atmospheric gases (O₂, H₂S, SO₂, etc.) forming protective or non-protective films.

Wet/Electrochemical Corrosion:

Occurs in presence of electrolyte. Includes galvanic, concentration cell, hydrogen evolution, oxygen absorption mechanisms.

Anodic Reaction: M → Mⁿ⁺ + n e⁻

Cathodic Reaction: H⁺ + e⁻ → H₂ (absence of oxygen) / 4H⁺ + O₂ + 4e⁻ → 2H₂O (presence of oxygen)

  • Protective coatings (metallic & non-metallic)
  • Cathodic protection
  • Material selection (resistant alloys)
  • Environmental control (humidity, pollutants)

Case Studies

Case Study 1: Rusting of Steel Fencing Wire at Joints

  1. Type of corrosion: Wet (Galvanic)
  2. Causes: Connection of steel with moisture exposure
  3. Preventive measures: Zinc coating, avoid metal contact
  4. Protection method: Sacrificial anode
  5. Wet or Dry: Wet

Case Study 2: Copper Sheets Joined with Iron Nails

  1. Type of corrosion: Galvanic
  2. Causes: Dissimilar metals in electrolyte
  3. Preventive measures: Use same metals, coating
  4. Protection method: Cathodic protection
  5. Wet or Dry: Wet

Case Study 3: Concentration Cell Corrosion in Pipelines

  1. Type of corrosion: Wet (Concentration Cell)
  2. Causes: Uneven oxygen concentration along pipe
  3. Preventive measures: Ensure uniform aeration, coating
  4. Protection method: Sacrificial anode
  5. Wet or Dry: Wet

Case Study 4: Hydrogen Embrittlement in High-Temperature Steel

  1. Type of corrosion: Hydrogen attack
  2. Causes: Atomic hydrogen penetration at high temperature
  3. Preventive measures: Proper alloy selection, heat treatment
  4. Protection method: Barrier coatings
  5. Wet or Dry: Wet

Case Study 5: Liquid Metal Attack in Nuclear Devices

  1. Type of corrosion: Liquid metal corrosion
  2. Causes: Chemical action of flowing liquid metal at high temperature
  3. Preventive measures: Use corrosion-resistant metals
  4. Protection method: Protective coatings
  5. Wet or Dry: Wet
